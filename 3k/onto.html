Definition of onto preposition from the Oxford Advanced Learner's Dictionary

onto

preposition
 
 
/ˈɒntə/, before vowels
 
/ˈɒntu/
 
/ˈɑːntə/,
 
/ˈɑːntu/
(also on to)
Phrasal Verbs
jump to other results
  1.  
     used with verbs to express movement on or to a particular place or position
    • Move the books onto the second shelf.
    • She stepped down from the train onto the platform.

    Questions about grammar and vocabulary?

    Find the answers with Practical English Usage online, your indispensable guide to problems in English.
  2.  
     used to show that something faces in a particular direction
    • The window looked out onto the terrace.
