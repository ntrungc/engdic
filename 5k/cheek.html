Definition of cheek noun from the Oxford Advanced Learner's Dictionary

cheek

noun
 
 
/tʃiːk/
 
/tʃiːk/
Idioms
jump to other results
  1. enlarge image
     
    [countable] either side of the face below the eyes
    • chubby/rosy/pink cheeks
    • He kissed her on both cheeks.
    • Couples were dancing cheek to cheek.

  3. [countable] (informal) either of the buttocks
  4. [uncountable, singular] (British English) talk or behaviour that people think is annoying, rude or not showing enough respect synonym nerve

    • I think they've got a cheek making you pay to park the car.

Idioms
cheek by jowl (with somebody/something)
  1. very close to somebody/something
    • The guests, packed cheek by jowl, parted as he entered.
put roses in somebody’s cheeks
  1. (British English, informal) to make somebody look healthy
turn the other cheek
  1. to make a deliberate decision to remain calm and not to act in an aggressive way when somebody has hurt you or made you angry
with your tongue in your cheek | with tongue in cheek
  1. if you say something with your tongue in your cheek, you are not being serious and mean it as a joke
