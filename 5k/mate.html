Definition of mate noun from the Oxford Advanced Learner's Dictionary

mate

noun
 
 
/meɪt/
 
/meɪt/
jump to other results

    friend

  1.  
    [countable] (British English, Australian English, informal) a friend
    • They've been best mates since school.
    • I was with a mate.
    Wordfinder
    • acquaintance
    • bond
    • buddy
    • companion
    • comrade
    • friend
    • mate
    • neighbour
    • platonic
    • playmate
    Extra Examples
    • He's a cheat and a gambler; hardly an ideal mate.
    • He's got loads of mates at school.
    Topics Family and relationshipsb2
    Oxford Collocations Dictionaryadjective
    • best
    • good
    verb + mate
    • have
    See full entry

    Questions about grammar and vocabulary?

    Find the answers with Practical English Usage online, your indispensable guide to problems in English.

    2. friendly name

  2.  
    [countable] (British English, Australian English, informal) used as a friendly way of addressing somebody, especially between men
    • Sorry mate, you'll have to wait.
    • All right, mate?
    3.

    somebody you share with

  3.  
    [countable] (in compounds) a person you share an activity or accommodation with
    • workmates/teammates/playmates/classmates
    • my room-mate/flatmate
    see also age-mate, running mate, soulmate

    4. bird/animal

  4.  
    [countable] the sexual partner of a bird or animal
    • A male bird sings to attract a mate.
    Topics Birdsb2, Animalsb2

    5. sexual partner

  5. [countable] (informal) a husband, wife or other sexual partner
    • Many matchmaking sites compile lists of potential mates using basic information.
    Oxford Collocations Dictionaryadjective
    • potential
    • prospective
    • ideal
    • …
    verb + mate
    • attract
    • find
    • choose
    • …
    See full entry
    6.

    job

  6. [countable] (British English) a person whose job is to help a worker who has had training in a skill
    • a builder’s/plumber’s mate
    7.

    on ship

  7. [countable] an officer in a commercial ship below the rank of captain or master see also first mateTopics Transport by waterc2

    8. in chess

  8. (also checkmate)
     [uncountable] (in chess) a position in which one player cannot prevent their king (= the most important piece) being captured and therefore loses the game
  9. (also checkmate)
     [uncountable] a situation in which somebody has been completely defeated
    10. Word Originnoun senses 1 to 7 late Middle English: from Middle Low German māt(e) ‘comrade’, of West Germanic origin; related to meat (the underlying concept was of eating together). noun sense 8 Middle English: the noun from Anglo-Norman French mat (from the phrase eschec mat ‘checkmate’); the verb from Anglo-Norman French mater ‘to checkmate’.
