Idioms Phrasal Verbs
jump to other results
  1.  
    [transitive] screw something + adv./prep. to fasten one thing to another or make something tight with a screw or screws
    • The bookcase is screwed to the wall.
    • You need to screw all the parts together.
    • Now screw down the lid.
    compare unscrew
    Extra Examples
    • She screwed the lock into the door.
    • The bed was screwed to the floor.

  2.  
    [transitive] to turn something round and round in order to fasten it in place
    • screw something + adv./prep. She screwed the cap back on the jar.
    • screw something + adj. Screw the bolt tight.
    compare unscrewTopics Engineeringc1

  3.  
    [intransitive] + adv./prep. to be attached by screwing
    • The bulb should just screw into the socket.
    • The lid simply screws on.
  4. [transitive] to use your hand to make something, especially a piece of paper, form a tight ball
    • screw something up (into something) I screwed up the letter and threw it into the fire.
    • screw something (up) into something Screw the foil into a little ball.
    see also screwed-up
  5. [transitive] (slang) to cheat somebody, especially by making them pay too much money for something
    • screw somebody We've been screwed.
    • screw somebody for something How much did they screw you for (= how much did you have to pay)?
  6. [transitive] screw something from/out of somebody (informal) to force somebody to give you something
    • They screwed the money out of her by threats.
  7. [intransitive, transitive] screw (somebody) (taboo, slang) to have sex with somebody
Idioms
have your head screwed on (the right way)
  1. (informal) to be a sensible person
screw him, you, that, etc.
  1. (taboo, offensive, slang) an offensive way of showing that you are annoyed or do not care about somebody/something
screw up your courage
  1. to force yourself to be brave enough to do something
    • I finally screwed up my courage and went to the dentist.
